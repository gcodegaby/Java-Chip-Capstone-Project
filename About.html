ABOUT US

Our Team

At our non-profit organization dedicated to combating sexual abuse in Austin, our staff comprises passionate individuals committed to providing support, advocacy, and resources to survivors and their communities. Each member of our team brings unique expertise and dedication to our cause.

From counselors offering compassionate guidance to legal advocates fighting for justice, and outreach coordinators spreading awareness, every staff member plays a vital role in our mission. Together, we strive to create a safe and supportive environment where survivors can heal, thrive, and access the resources they need to rebuild their lives.

Our History

45 years ago, women’s advocates in Austin established the Austin Rape Crisis Center and the Center for Battered Women, later merging to form SafePlace in 1998. SafePlace became nationally recognized for its innovative programs. Concurrently, concerned citizens founded the Austin-Travis County Shelter for Infants and Children in 1984, which evolved into the Austin Children’s Shelter.

In 2015, SafePlace and Austin Children’s Shelter merged to form SAFE, dedicated to ending violence and abuse. Accredited by the Council on Accreditation in 2021, SAFE operates prevention and crisis programs with an annual budget exceeding $20 million and over 350 employees and volunteers, drawing a line against violence and abuse in Austin.

Our Mission

Our mission is in our name: Stop Abuse For Everyone. At SAFE, we are dedicated to fostering respectful and trusting relationships while championing diversity, accessibility, inclusiveness, and a sense of belonging for all.

Our unwavering focus is on survivors, ensuring their empowerment and well-being through mutual accountability and shared responsibility. Through collaboration and continuous learning, we adapt and innovate to create safer environments for individuals and our organization alike. At SAFE, safety is paramount, and we celebrate every step towards a community free from abuse and violence.

Our Goals & Projects

In the upcoming year, SAFE is embarking on several impactful projects to address critical issues in our community. Recognizing the link between affordable housing and violence prevention, we are committed to expanding access to safe and affordable housing options for vulnerable individuals and families.

Additionally, we are prioritizing initiatives aimed at ensuring safe home placement for children affected by abuse and neglect, providing them with the stability and support they need to thrive. Central to our mission is our ongoing efforts to end the cycle of abuse through comprehensive programming and support services, working tirelessly to create a safer and more compassionate community for all.
