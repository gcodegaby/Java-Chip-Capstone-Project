ABOUT US

Our team

At our non-profit organization dedicated to combating sexual abuse in Austin, our staff comprises passionate individuals committed to providing support, advocacy, and resources to survivors and their communities. Each member of our team brings unique expertise and dedication to our cause.

From counselors offering compassionate guidance to legal advocates fighting for justice, and outreach coordinators spreading awareness, every staff member plays a vital role in our mission. Together, we strive to create a safe and supportive environment where survivors can heal, thrive, and access the resources they need to rebuild their lives.

Our history

45 years ago, women’s advocates in Austin established the Austin Rape Crisis Center and the Center for Battered Women, later merging to form SafePlace in 1998. SafePlace became nationally recognized for its innovative programs. Concurrently, concerned citizens founded the Austin-Travis County Shelter for Infants and Children in 1984, which evolved into the Austin Children’s Shelter.

In 2015, SafePlace and Austin Children’s Shelter merged to form SAFE, dedicated to ending violence and abuse. Accredited by the Council on Accreditation in 2021, SAFE operates prevention and crisis programs with an annual budget exceeding $20 million and over 350 employees and volunteers, drawing a line against violence and abuse in Austin.
